Aug 30, 2020

Welcome to the ESP Spanish Wiki! Here’s some essential knowledge to kick-start your Spanish learning journey!

Gender#

Spanish nouns have gender. Usually, masculine nouns end in -o, like /, and feminine nouns end in -a. This is a general pattern and you will see many exceptions to it. Other patterns will emerge as you learn more Spanish.

Subject Pronouns#

Like many other languages, you need to conjugate a verb from its infinitive form (e.g. to see in English or ver in Spanish) in order to use it (e.g. sees in English and veo in Spanish). How you conjugate the verb depends on the person taking the action, which can be represented by the following types of subject pronouns.

Category / PoV Singular Plural
First person yo = I nosotros, nosotras
Second person (informal) tú (informal) = You vosotros
Third person él = he, ella = she ellos = they, ellas = they (females only)
